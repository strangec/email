I feel really strongly about not reading this poem and the painful disappointment it houses-—in addition to hopeful speculation—-as a call for presidential reform, but rather as a testament to the historical function of “presidency” as a term: a material investment in militarized hierarchy. A measure of distance created and maintained. Still I would argue that Leonard’s poem offers an act of re-programming; presidency shapeshifts as it is re-inhabited and thus re-coded by Leonard’s imagination.
